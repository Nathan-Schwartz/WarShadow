Kills per Minute

This feature outputs a ratio of Kills divided by Minutes rounded to nearest hundredths place. The display is updated for each kill as well as every second. This allows higher accuracy than simply updating every minute.

The time starts as soon as the K/min window is opened and can be reset using the "Reset Counters" hotkey or via the button in Settings.

# Headshot

This feature simply displays the number of headshots achieved by the player. The counter starts when WarShadow launches and can be reset using the "Reset Counters" hotkey or via the button in Settings.

% Headshot

This feature displays the result of: <# of Headshots>/<# of Kills> X 100. It is updated after each kill. The counter starts when WarShadow launches and can be reset using the "Reset Counters" hotkey or via the button in Settings.

HS Streak Average

This feature shows how many headshots the player gets in quick succession on average.
Examples: If a player only got brain annhilators the window would always display "5 Avg". If a player never got a skull hunter, head crasher, brain smasher, or brain annhilator the window would always display "1 Avg".

The counter starts when WarShadow launches and can be reset using the "Reset Counters" hotkey or via the button in Settings.

Recording Counter

It isn't always easy to keep track of how many videos you have recorded when you are using Auto-Record so I made a counter that tells you exactly that. It does not reset when you reset the other counters. It starts at zero every time WarShadow launches.

Crosshair

This feature allows the user to use a custom crosshair in Warface. The user can use preloaded crosshairs or upload their own. Only shows up while in-game.

Setting it up

Open the Settings page and scroll down to the Crosshair section. If you use right click to aim down sights, make sure the box is checked. Select Hold-to-Aim or Toggle-Aim so that it is the same as your Warface settings. If you use Right Click to ADS you are done!
If you use a custom key to aim, click "Grab ADS" and push your ADS key. To make sure that the app recieved the correct key, push it again. If the text below the button flashes grey you are all finished!

General Recording Info

Q: Does the recording capability save past gameplay like ShadowPlay or does it do start-stop recording like more traditional programs?
A: Both. Auto-Recording and Manual Capture both save your past game-play, but you can do manual start-stop recordings as well.

Q: Why won't the Auto-Recording box stay checked?
A: It is designed to uncheck itself if the recording process didn't start correctly. If it ever stays clicked, it is working. This error often happens because it was clicked when the user wasn't in-game. If this wasn't the issue, try clicking the close button and restarting the app. Read more in the Problems section below.

Q: Why does the program lag when I click some of the recording buttons?
A: These buttons are starting the recording background process and it takes a few seconds for Overwolf to get everything ready.

Q: What is a pending recording, and why do I have one?
A: Do you remember the slider in Settings that lets you choose how much video you wanted to save after the auto-Recording event happened? When an Auto-Record event is triggered, my app waits that long before saving the video. It is considered "pending" until it actually saves the video. The reason for this is that only one recording can happen at a time. If I didn't wait till after the time was finished, the other Auto-Recording events would be unsuccessful because the first recording is hogging the feature.

Q: Why should I use Warshadow for recording instead of ReplayHUD?
A: 3 reasons.
1) "Auto-Recording", it takes all the recording for you so you can focus on playing the game.
2) The option to do longer videos. ReplayHUD currently only allows 40 second videos. Mine does 3 minutes for Auto-Recording, but I haven't found a time limit for start-stop recording.
3) No recharge time. ReplayHUD currently makes you wait 40 seconds before taking another recording.

Auto-Recording

This is probably the most useful feature in my app. AutoRec records your best moments for you, so you can focus on playing the game. No more massive recordings that you comb through later and no more fumbling for the "Save video" Hotkey.

Simply select which moments you want to save (this is done in settings) and click on Auto-Record in the main menu. Whenever your chosen events happen, the app will save the video recording for you and you never have to worry about it.

You can choose to change the length of the recording. Don't worry about making the times too short, the app has a built in time for each event so that even if you say you want a 2 second recording, you will still get video of your whole brain annihilator.

It is recommended to have a longer "after" duration if you choose a short combo. Example: If you choose to record all of the 2x headshot chains but you get 5 in a row, the video might not save the last kills.

Recording Menu

A low profile, simple menu that has all the controls you need to record and view your gameplay. Note that disabling the recording will uncheck the Auto-Record checkbox.

Problems and Troubleshooting

Some people are going to have errors with the recording feature. This can be due to hardware limitations, out of date graphics card, not enough memory, or other reasons.

Here are a few solutions that have solved Overwolf recording issues in the past.
- Only close the app with the Close button. This sounds silly, but the button will disable the recording process before exiting the app, so it can help prevent conflicts.
- Double check your Video settings to make sure they aren't too demanding for your machine.
- Make sure you have enough space for a recording.
- Check for graphics card updates.
- Restart your computer.
If none of those options work, you may consider reinstalling Overwolf but this not a guaranteed fix either.

The bottom line is that I'm sorry you are having an error, but I may not be able to help you. Unfortunately I haven't been provided with a full list of errors or their fixes so I have no way of knowing what will work for your specific error, nor a way to fix this error should you have it :( I will strive to keep the application up to date so everyone has a positive experience with it. If you would like to make suggestions, or if you encounter any problems that you believe to be my apps fault, I ask that you contact me at WarShadowDev@gmail.com.

Where do you get the data?
I maintain plausible deniability regarding the acquisition of any damage related data. That said, I have a friend who has been tremendously helpful with this aspect of the app and I have the utmost confidence in the data's accuracy.

Why is the damage graph on Shotguns so weird? I'm so glad you asked! Its because I made it up. Kinda. All of the shotguns in the game have a certain number of pellets. That is what makes shotguns great! The catch is that my calculations assumes that the user has 100% accuracy.

That becomes a problem because at long distances it isn't possible to shoot the enemy with ALL of the pellets (without hacks). Since there is no real way for me to predict the spread patterns I made up a formula:
Every 1 meter (roughly) the chance of hitting with all pellets reduces by 1%. Then I use that % of the guns pellets in the calculation.

Thats why the graph is all bumpy, at that distance 1 less pellet hit the enemy.

Ok so then what good are the shotgun calculations?
I had this question myself, and I decided that the chart is only good for comparing shotguns against other shotguns. Comparing against any other weapon is just a guess.

This section is to go over some common general issues and questions regarding both my App and the Overwolf Overlay.

Public Service Announcement:
There are currently some incompatibilities with Cyber Horde. Kamikazes don't trigger the Overwolf "kill" event and Headshot Combos don't trigger either. There is nothing I can do about it since it would have to be fixed by Warface.

Help! The buttons on the App won't click and they keep flickering when I mouse over them
To fix this, click on the game, and then back on the App menu. A more permanent solution is to got to the Overwolf Settings Page on the General tab and change "Overwolf In-Game Control" to "Hitting Ctrl+Tab".

The mouse is on the screen when I'm playing and it won't go away.
This can happen when you alt-tab sometimes. Usually this means that you need to relaunch either your game or Overwolf.

The Overwolf overlay (and Warshadow) are not popping up in my game!!
This is probably because your game was running before Overwolf was. You need to restart your game and perhaps Overwolf.

Can I choose to make Warshadow not show up in my videos?
Yes. Navigate to Overwolf Settings > Capture and then change the setting Recording Mode from monitor to active game. Unselect "Capture Overwolf windows". You are all set!

Overwolf says I can't edit the Video Settings while I am recording
Thats a quick one. Go to the WarShadow Main menu and then launch the Recording menu. Click the power button to turn off the recording background process and you will be able to edit the settings again!
